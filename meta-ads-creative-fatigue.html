How creative fatigue unfolds

The silent degradation timeline.

Creative fatigue doesn't announce itself. It builds quietly across 3–7 days — and most brands only notice at stage 4.

Day 1–2 · Early signal

Frequency starts climbing

Your audience has seen the creative 2–3 times. CTR begins a mild downward trend — easily confused with normal variance. Most dashboards won't flag this yet.

Day 3–4 · Degradation accelerates

CTR drops 15–25%

Frequency is now above 4×. The algorithm compensates by serving to lower-quality audiences. CPA starts rising. Spend continues at the same rate — into worsening performance.

Day 5–7 · Full fatigue

ROAS collapses

CPA is now 40–60% above baseline. ROAS has fallen sharply. The Meta algorithm has allocated budget to audiences that convert poorly. Manual review finally catches it — days too late.

The 3 signals crumplz monitors

Early warning before ROAS follows.

Creative fatigue leaves a footprint across three metrics before it hits your bottom line. crumplz tracks all three simultaneously.

Signal 1

CTR degradation

Click-through rate is the first fatigue signal. A 10–15% decline vs the 7-day ad set baseline is an early indicator — long before CPA is affected. crumplz tracks CTR per ad set with dynamic baselines that account for day-of-week patterns.

Signal 2

Frequency threshold breach

When frequency exceeds 3.5–4× for a cold or retargeting audience, creative wear-out accelerates. crumplz flags frequency spikes alongside CTR trends so you can distinguish fatigue from algorithmic variance.

Signal 3

CPA trend (not just a single spike)

A single high-CPA day can be noise. crumplz monitors the trend — 3 consecutive monitoring cycles of rising CPA on the same ad set triggers a creative fatigue alert rather than a random anomaly flag.

Why this matters

Creative fatigue is the #1 cause of silent ROAS decay.

The compounding problem

Unlike a sudden ROAS crash — which is hard to miss — creative fatigue is gradual. Each day the metrics are slightly worse than the day before. Without automated detection, most brands notice only when CPA has already spiked significantly above target.

Why manual reviews miss it

Manual reviews happen at check-in cadences of once a day or less. The 4h17 average detection delay observed across DTC brands is largely a creative fatigue problem: the signal was present but the monitoring wasn't frequent enough to catch it early.

Why crumplz doesn't false-positive

crumplz cross-validates every creative fatigue alert against Shopify MER. If overall marketing efficiency is holding, an isolated CTR decline might be a targeting or offer problem — not a creative one. crumplz distinguishes between the two before alerting.

FAQ

Creative fatigue questions answered.

How is crumplz's creative fatigue detection different from Meta's built-in frequency data?
Meta's Ads Manager shows you frequency as a static number. crumplz monitors the trend across multiple metrics simultaneously — CTR, frequency, and CPA together — and cross-validates against your Shopify MER before alerting. You get a synthesised diagnosis rather than raw data to interpret yourself.
Can crumplz tell me which specific creatives are fatigued?
crumplz identifies the ad set level where fatigue is occurring and highlights the metrics driving the signal. It can isolate whether the pattern is consistent with creative fatigue (high frequency, CTR decline) vs other causes (audience exhaustion, offer mismatch, landing page issues).
Will I get alerts for every small CTR fluctuation?
No. crumplz uses dynamic thresholds calibrated to each ad set's historical variance. Normal daily fluctuations are filtered out. An alert fires only when multiple signals align — CTR decline, frequency trend, and CPA movement — across consecutive monitoring cycles. False positive prevention is a core part of the product.

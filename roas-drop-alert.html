How it works

Dynamic ROAS monitoring,
not static thresholds.

Dynamic baseline, not a fixed number

Most tools let you set "alert when ROAS < 2x." The problem: a ROAS of 2x on a Thursday is different from 2x on Black Friday. crumplz builds a dynamic baseline per campaign from your historical data — so it flags real deviations, not seasonal noise.

Shopify MER cross-validation

Meta's reported ROAS can lag or over-attribute. Before alerting, crumplz cross-checks your Meta signals against your real Shopify revenue and MER. If Shopify confirms the drop, the alert fires. If it's attribution lag, it holds.

Catches ROAS drift, not just ROAS floor

A ROAS that drops 40% in 2 hours is a different signal from a ROAS that's been slowly declining over 3 days. crumplz detects both drift velocity (sudden drop) and trend degradation (slow bleed), with different alert severities.

Recommended action in every alert

Every ROAS alert includes a specific recommended move — not just a data point. "Cut budget −30% for 12h and recheck" or "Rotate creative: relaunch top 3 ads from last 14 days." The decision is yours, but the action is ready.

FAQ

About ROAS drop alerts

How quickly does crumplz detect a ROAS drop?
crumplz runs a full check every 2 hours. So the maximum lag between a ROAS drop happening and you receiving the alert is 2 hours. In practice, most teams using crumplz react within 20 minutes of receiving the alert — vs the industry average of 4h17 without an alert system.
Will I get false alarms on normal fluctuations?
crumplz is designed to minimize false positives. Dynamic baselines account for day-of-week patterns and campaign history. And before alerting on a ROAS drop, crumplz cross-validates against Shopify MER — if Shopify revenue is holding, it holds the alert. The goal is one real signal, not a flood of noise.
Can I set my own ROAS alert thresholds?
Yes. On the Growth and Pro plans you can set custom thresholds per campaign — for example "alert when ROAS drops more than 35% from the 7-day average" or "alert if ROAS falls below 2.5x for more than 4 hours." Starter uses crumplz's automatic dynamic thresholds.
Does crumplz monitor ROAS at campaign, ad set, or ad level?
crumplz monitors at campaign and ad set level by default. Campaign-level monitoring catches systemic drift; ad set level catches audience or placement-specific issues. Ad-level monitoring is on the roadmap for the Pro plan.

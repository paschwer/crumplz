What is MER and why does it matter for DTC?

MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio) is total revenue divided by total ad spend — a cleaner signal of real business profitability than Meta's reported ROAS, which can lag, over-attribute, or miss post-iOS14 gaps. crumplz cross-validates Meta's signals against your live Shopify MER to filter out false positives and catch drifts that Meta's own platform doesn't flag.

If Meta says ROAS is holding but Shopify revenue is declining, crumplz flags the discrepancy. If MER confirms the drift, the alert fires. If Shopify is fine and it's a Meta attribution lag, it holds. Fewer false positives. More real signals.

Does crumplz work with any Shopify store?
Yes. crumplz connects to any Shopify store via read-only OAuth. It pulls revenue, AOV and conversion rate data to cross-validate your Meta Ads signals. No apps to install on your Shopify store — just a standard API connection.
What if I run multiple Shopify stores?
The Pro plan supports multi-store and multiple Meta Ads accounts, with roles and permissions so each brand or client has their own alert stream. The Growth plan covers up to 3 ad accounts.
Can crumplz make changes to my campaigns automatically?
No. crumplz is read-only by design. It detects drifts and recommends actions, but the decision — and the click — stays with you. Budget automation is on the Pro roadmap as an opt-in feature, but the default is always human-in-the-loop.
What's the minimum spend level for crumplz to be useful?
crumplz makes the most financial sense when one drift incident (average ~€380 lost) costs more than the monthly subscription. That's typically around €5k–10k/month in ad spend. Below that threshold, the math still works — but the urgency is lower.

The 4 causes of high CPA

Same symptom. Four different problems.

Treating the wrong cause makes things worse. Here's how to tell them apart — and what crumplz checks to diagnose each one.

01

Creative fatigue

Your audience has seen these ads too many times. CTR declines first, then conversion rate, then CPA spikes. The tell: high frequency (3.5×+) alongside the CPA rise. Shopify MER may hold if other channels are performing.

Rotate creatives. Introduce new hooks, formats, or offers. Reduce frequency cap.
02

Audience exhaustion

Your targetable audience has been fully saturated. Unlike creative fatigue, refreshing creatives won't fix it. The tell: high frequency but CTR still reasonable — the remaining audience just doesn't convert as well as the early movers.

Expand audience. Test broader targeting, lookalikes, or new interest segments.
03

Algorithm instability

Meta's delivery system enters an unstable phase after budget changes, audience edits, or creative swaps. CPA spikes 2–3× for 24–72 hours while the algorithm re-optimises. The tell: no changes to creative or audience but sudden CPA spike with CTR stable.

Wait 48–72 hours before intervening. Do not make further changes — they reset the learning phase.
04

Landing page / offer degradation

Click-through is fine but conversion rate dropped. Something changed downstream from the ad — the landing page, the offer, the price point, checkout friction. The tell: CTR and CPM are stable but CPC-to-conversion rate has fallen sharply.

Check landing page load speed, offer clarity, and checkout flow. Restore the previous landing page version if recently changed.

FAQ

High CPA questions.

How does crumplz know which cause is driving my CPA spike?
crumplz cross-references multiple signals simultaneously: CTR trend, frequency, impression volume, CPM, conversion rate, and Shopify MER. The pattern of which signals are moving — and in which direction — points to one of the four causes. It's the same diagnostic framework a performance marketer uses manually, automated and run every 2 hours.
What's the difference between a CPA spike and a CPA trend?
A CPA spike is a single-period anomaly — CPA is high for one check but normalises. This can be noise. A CPA trend is a consistent direction across multiple monitoring cycles — this is actionable. crumplz distinguishes between the two: spikes are logged but don't trigger alerts unless the pattern persists across 2–3 cycles.
My CPA is high but ROAS is fine — should I be worried?
This can happen when average order value increases — more expensive items are converting, pushing CPA up while ROAS holds or improves. crumplz's Shopify MER cross-validation catches this: if revenue per order is higher, the apparent CPA spike is not a problem. The alert won't fire unless the MER signal confirms real efficiency loss.

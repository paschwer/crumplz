Where budget goes to waste

The 4 types of budget drain.

Meta Ads budget waste is rarely one dramatic moment. It's four slow leaks running simultaneously — undetected.

Type 1

Performance drift

ROAS or CPA slides gradually from baseline. Spend continues at the same rate into worsening returns. Without monitoring, this persists for days before anyone notices.

Type 2

Creative fatigue overspend

Fatigued creatives keep spending at full budget while CTR declines. The algorithm hunts for marginal audiences to fill delivery — at worse CPA. Budget burns on exhausted assets.

Type 3

Budget pacing mismatch

Campaign underspends early in the month, then overcorrects and overspends in the final week. The net effect: budget is concentrated in low-quality inventory windows.

Type 4

Algorithm instability waste

Meta's algorithm periodically enters unstable learning phases that spike CPA 2–3× without clear creative or audience cause. Without early detection, these phases drain budget for 24–72 hours undetected.

FAQ

Budget protection questions.

Can crumplz automatically pause overspending campaigns?
No — and this is by design. crumplz is read-only. It cannot pause, modify, or take any action on your campaigns. It detects, diagnoses, and alerts. You decide whether to act and how. This eliminates automation risk — no campaign paused by mistake, no budget cut at the wrong moment. Human judgment stays in the loop.
How does crumplz know what "normal" spend looks like for my account?
crumplz builds dynamic baselines from your account's own history. It accounts for day-of-week variance (weekends typically spend differently), recent trend changes, and campaign lifecycle stage. Thresholds are not static — they adapt as your account's patterns evolve. This prevents both missed alerts and false alarms.
What if my budget spike is intentional — like a flash sale?
crumplz cross-validates budget anomalies against ROAS and MER. If spend is up but ROAS is holding or improving, the alert will note the pacing overage but flag it as likely intentional. You can also add campaign notes inside crumplz to mark periods where elevated spend is expected — these notes suppress budget drift alerts for that window.

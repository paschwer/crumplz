FAQ

About choosing a
Meta Ads monitoring tool

Do I need a separate monitoring tool if I have Triple Whale or Northbeam?
If your attribution platform alerts you proactively when ROAS drifts — with a recommended action — then no. If it shows you the data when you open it, yes. Most analytics tools are pull (you go to them). crumplz is push (it comes to you). That difference matters at 11pm on a Thursday.
What is "dynamic anomaly detection" and why does it matter?
Static thresholds ("alert when CPA > €40") trigger false positives during seasonality and miss gradual drift. Dynamic thresholds learn your campaign's historical pattern and flag deviations — so an alert on a Monday is calibrated differently from one on a Black Friday. crumplz uses dynamic, per-campaign thresholds to reduce noise and catch real drifts.
What does crumplz NOT do?
crumplz does not do multi-touch attribution, creative analytics, cohort reporting, lifetime value modelling, or campaign management. It is not a dashboard. It watches your Meta Ads, detects drifts, and tells you what to do. The decision stays with you.
What ad spend level does crumplz make sense for?
crumplz is built for DTC Shopify brands spending €5k to €200k/month on Meta Ads. The Starter plan covers up to ~€10k/month. Growth covers €10k–50k. Pro covers €50k–200k and supports multi-store and multiple ad accounts. Below €5k, the cost of a drift incident is usually lower than the subscription — but many brands start early to build the habit.

What crumplz monitors

Full spend coverage in one tool.

crumplz covers every dimension of ad spend health — not just whether you're over budget, but whether the spend is working.

Budget velocity

Is spend pacing on track for the day, week, and month? crumplz flags overspend and underspend trajectories before they become month-end surprises.

ROAS monitoring

Dynamic baselines per ad set. ROAS decline below the expected range triggers an alert — distinguishing real drift from normal day-to-day variance.

CPA trend tracking

CPA spikes identified across 3 consecutive monitoring cycles — with root cause diagnosis included in every alert: creative fatigue, audience exhaustion, algorithm, or landing page.

CTR degradation

The earliest signal of creative fatigue. CTR decline is tracked per ad set with frequency cross-referencing to distinguish fatigue from demand changes.

Shopify MER validation

Every alert is cross-validated against Shopify Marketing Efficiency Ratio. Channel-level drops offset by other channel performance won't generate false alarms.

Multi-account coverage

Monitor multiple Meta Ads accounts from a single crumplz workspace — each with its own baselines. Alerts are tagged by account so you always know where the issue is.

FAQ

Ad spend monitoring questions.

Is crumplz an attribution platform or a monitoring tool?
crumplz is specifically a monitoring and alerting tool — not an attribution platform. It doesn't try to attribute revenue to channels or provide cross-channel analytics. Its job is to detect when something is drifting in your Meta Ads and tell you about it — with context — before budget is wasted. If you need attribution, tools like Triple Whale or Northbeam do that. crumplz watches over everything and alerts you when intervention is needed.
Does crumplz work alongside other monitoring or attribution tools?
Yes — crumplz is designed to complement other tools. Many brands use attribution platforms for post-campaign analysis and crumplz for real-time monitoring. The two are not redundant: attribution tools tell you what happened, crumplz tells you what's happening right now and whether you need to act.
How does crumplz handle multi-currency ad accounts?
crumplz monitors each ad account in its native currency and compares performance against that account's own historical baselines. Cross-account comparisons use a normalised efficiency ratio rather than absolute spend figures, so multi-currency setups are handled correctly.

Why CPA spikes happen

The 5 most common causes of
sudden CPA spikes on Meta.

crumplz catches all of them — and tells you which one it likely is.

Creative fatigue

Your top creative stops resonating. CTR drops, cost-per-click rises, and Meta keeps spending. The CPA climbs until you rotate the creative.

Audience exhaustion

Your lookalike or retargeting audience has seen your ad too many times. Frequency rises, conversion rate drops, CPA spikes.

Algorithm instability

Meta's learning phase or a competitor's surge in the same audience pushes your CPM up. CPA follows without any change on your end.

Landing page issues

A Shopify theme update, slow load time, or broken checkout flow cuts your conversion rate. Meta keeps spending at full CPA on zero conversions.

How crumplz monitors CPA

Dynamic CPA baseline per campaign

crumplz doesn't use a single static "alert when CPA > €X" rule. It builds a rolling baseline per campaign from your historical data, accounting for day-of-week and spend level — so it flags real anomalies, not normal fluctuations.

Shopify conversion rate cross-check

A CPA spike can come from rising click costs or falling conversion rates. crumplz cross-validates against your Shopify conversion rate and revenue data to distinguish between a Meta delivery problem and a landing page problem.

CPA + ROAS + MER together

CPA in isolation can be misleading. crumplz monitors CPA alongside ROAS and MER simultaneously — so a CPA spike that's offset by higher AOV doesn't trigger a false alarm, but a CPA spike with MER decline does.

Actionable alert, not just a data point

FAQ

About CPA spike alerts

How does crumplz know if a CPA spike is real or just noise?
Dynamic thresholds, Shopify cross-validation, and multi-metric confirmation. crumplz doesn't alert on CPA alone — it confirms that the spike is corroborated by a meaningful change in MER, CVR, or ROAS before alerting. This filters out statistical noise and one-off check-out abandonment spikes.
Can crumplz tell me why my CPA spiked?
crumplz can identify the likely signal pattern — for example, "CTR stable but CVR dropped, likely landing page or checkout issue" vs "CTR dropped and CPM up, likely audience exhaustion or bid competition." It can't diagnose root causes inside your Shopify theme, but it narrows the investigation significantly.
What's the recommended action when CPA spikes?
Every crumplz alert includes a recommended first action based on the signal pattern. Examples: "Pause Audience Network placements (24h test)" for delivery issues, "Rotate creative — relaunch top 3 from last 14 days" for creative fatigue, "Check Shopify checkout flow" for CVR-driven spikes. These are recommendations, not automated actions — you always decide.
